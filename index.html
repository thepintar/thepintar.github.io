My journey, like many, began on the Oregon Trail, a step behind the rippling red trench coat of Carmen Sandiego, inside a classroom during recess inserting the floppiest of floppy disks into an old Apple IIe. I died a gumshoe stricken with dysentery out there on the range, but I was reborn with code running through my veins, a ten year old Neo, a new world open to me. I scrounged up ten dollars and bought my own Apple IIe at a rummage sale.

No one is quite certain about what happened next. Some say I died off the coast of Chile in a geographically unlikely waterspout, while others claim the American School System in the 90s was unhelpful in encouraging a child's interest in tech, and for a time I languished in an unrelated field. Whatever the case, after spending ten years working in media as a videographer and video editor, a move to Chicago gave me the opportunity to get back on that horse, and I am off galloping.

At Dev Bootcamp here in Chicago, I reconnected with my childlike curiosity about coding. I've taken all the creativity and passion I put into video editing and found it all operates exactly the same in the world of software development. I've rediscovered a passion for learning everything that I can about the field, which is vast. Lastly, I've found a real life Carmen Sandiego: a life-long pursuit of excellence where I'll learn a thing or two and have a blast along the way.

My work style is efficient, thorough, focused, and results-driven. My colleagues describe me as adaptable, resourceful, consistent, and eager to both get a laugh and the job done. I strongly value honesty, excellence, collaboration, and loyalty.

Duckies!

Duckies is a game based off of the popular "2048" and was a solo challenge I completed at DevBootcamp. It was one of the first javascript projects I undertook with emphasis on Object-oriented programming. As an extended challenge to myself I opted out of storing the values in a nested array, and instead stored them in one long array. The final code could use a refactor, but I believe it illustrates solid OOP.

Controls:

Up Arrow to shift all images up

Left Arrow to shift all images left

Down Arrow to shift all images down

Right Arrow to shift all images right
